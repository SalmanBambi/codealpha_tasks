What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyberattack where criminals impersonate trusted entities to steal sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or login credentials.

📧

Email Phishing

Mass emails pretending to be from banks, services like PayPal, or your employer asking you to click a link.

🎯

Spear Phishing

Highly targeted attacks using your name, job title, or personal details gathered from social media.

📱

Smishing

Phishing via SMS text messages. "Your package is on hold — click here to reschedule delivery."

📞

Vishing

Voice phishing — phone calls from fake tech support, IRS agents, or bank fraud departments.

🐳

Whaling

Targeting high-profile executives (CEOs, CFOs) for large financial transfers or sensitive data.

🌐

Pharming

Redirecting users from legitimate websites to fake ones without them clicking any link.

Spot the Red Flags

Below is a real-world style phishing email. Hover over the highlighted sections to see what makes them suspicious.

From: security-alert@paypa1.com⚑ FAKE DOMAIN
To: you@email.com
Subject: ⚠️ URGENT: Your account will be suspended in 24 hours!⚑ URGENCY TACTIC

Dear Valued Customer,


We have detected suspicious activity on your PayPal account. Your account has been temporarily limited.


To restore your account access, please verify your information immediately by clicking below:


Verify My Account Now

If you do not verify within 24 hours, your account will be permanently suspended.


Regards,
PayPal Security Team

⚑

paypa1.com ≠ paypal.com

Attackers swap letters with lookalike characters. Always check the sender domain character by character. paypa1 uses the number "1" instead of the letter "l".

⚑

Urgency + Fear = Manipulation

Phrases like "your account will be suspended", "act now", or "24 hours" are designed to panic you into clicking without thinking.

⚑

Generic Greetings

Legitimate companies know your name. "Dear Valued Customer" is a major red flag. Real service emails always address you by name.

How to Stay Safe

Follow these best practices to protect yourself and your organization from phishing attacks.

01

Verify the sender's email domain. Don't just check the display name — look at the actual email address. Hover over links before clicking to preview the real URL.

02

Never click links in unexpected emails. If your bank emails you, go directly to the bank's website by typing the URL yourself, not by clicking the email link.

03

Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA). Even if attackers steal your password, MFA prevents them from accessing your account without a second verification.

04

Slow down — urgency is a weapon. Attackers create panic to rush you. Real companies don't threaten you with 24-hour deadlines via email.

05

Check for HTTPS — but don't rely on it alone. The padlock means the connection is encrypted, not that the site is legitimate. Phishing sites can have HTTPS too.

06

Report suspicious emails. Use your email client's "Report Phishing" button. This protects you and helps train spam filters for everyone.

07

Keep software updated. Attackers exploit known vulnerabilities in outdated browsers and OS. Security patches often close these loopholes.

08

Use a password manager. Password managers won't autofill your credentials on a fake website — a powerful defence against lookalike domains.
